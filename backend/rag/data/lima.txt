Lima, the vibrant capital of Peru perched on the Pacific coast, is a city bursting with rich history and world-class culinary experiences. The best time to visit Lima is during the coastal summer from December to April, when the skies are clear and sunny, offering a beautiful break from the city's infamous winter fog. Top attractions you must explore include the historic center with its grand colonial architecture, the stunning oceanfront cliffs of Miraflores, and the bohemian district of Barranco, known for its colorful street art and the romantic Bridge of Sighs. Renowned as the gastronomic capital of South America, Lima offers unforgettable local food, most notably fresh ceviche cured in citrus juice, lomo saltado, and delicious sweet picarones for dessert. Navigating the city can be an adventure, so tourists find it easiest to get around by using the efficient Metropolitano bus rapid transit system to skip traffic, or by booking reliable ride-sharing apps like Uber and Cabify. For shorter distances, walking through the scenic parks of Miraflores is both safe and delightful, making Lima an unforgettable destination for any traveler.
